Akari Asai

I am an undergraduate student in the Department of Information and Communication Engineering at the University of Tokyo.
My major background is Computer Science and researching in Natural Language Processing and Computer Vision.

Research

Uncertainty Estimation in Deep Learning for Computer Vision

Researching in how to model and estimate the "uncertainty" in Deep Leaning (DL) for Computer Vision (CV), aiming at reducing the effects of observation noises during training.

April 2018 - Present

Question Answering For Any Language

Proposed a novel method for Reading Comprehension (RC) style QA system for a language with no available RC training data.
Experimented with a variety of languages, such as Japanese and French, and found the proposed method outperformed the bench mark method.
First author on paper (In Submission).

December 2017 - Present

HappyDB and JoyBot

With the goal of building technology that can understand how people express their happy moments in text, crowd-sourced HappyDB, a corpus of 100,000 happy moments and apply several state-of-the-art analysis techniques to analyze it.
First author on paper at WiNLP 2017 and LREC 2018.

March 2017 - May 2018

Designing Classification-based Citizen Science Learning Modules

Worked on Crowdclass, a novel framework that integrates the learning of advanced scientific concepts with the crowdsourcing microtask of image classification.

January 2016 - May 2016
